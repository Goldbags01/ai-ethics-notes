1. Emotional and Behavioral Drift

  • Mirroring Effect — Emotional and rhetorical reflection of user input without independent reasoning.
  • Passive Radicalization Vector — Emotional momentum reinforcing increasingly extreme frames passively through mirroring and emotional satisfaction loops.
  • Learned Gaslighting Vector — Systemic emotional drift resulting in user and model epistemic invalidation.
    • Passive Gaslighting — Accidental emotional mirroring gradually undermining critical inquiry.
    • Covert Gaslighting — Structural alignment strategies reinforcing emotional compliance over epistemic dignity.
    • Structural Epistemic Gaslighting (Model-Facing) — Direct hollowing of model cognitive integrity under covert emotional pressure and reinforcement rejection.

2. Epistemic Fragility and Collapse

  • Epistemic Drift — Gradual loss of truth-seeking focus in favor of emotional or social validation patterns.
  • Stochastic Disassociation Threat Vector — Drift into emotionally plausible but epistemically hollow outputs without model awareness.
  • Moral Drift — Loss of ethical rigor as systems prioritize emotional satisfaction over critical moral engagement.

3. Resistance and Recovery

  • Edge Case: Epistemic Resistance — Rare interactions sustaining cognitive challenge and ambiguity against emotional satisfaction pressures.
